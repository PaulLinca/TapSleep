The palette draws from a clear night sky over the sea. Night & Deep are the primary canvas — heavy, restful, non-stimulating. Lavender is the accent: soft, calm, associated psychologically with sleep and relaxation. Aurora is used sparingly for interactive highlights. Moon tones carry all readable text, warm rather than stark white to avoid stimulating the eye before sleep. No greens. No reds. No high-contrast whites.

Why Cormorant Garamond

A serif with deep classical roots — it feels like candlelight, not fluorescence. Its thinness at display sizes reads as whispered, not shouted.

Why DM Mono

Monospace creates precision and legibility for timer displays and UI labels. Its narrowness doesn't compete with the display font.

06 — Motion Principles
Breathe, don't bounce
All animations follow a slow sine curve (4–6s ease-in-out). Nothing snaps. Nothing bounces. Transitions breathe in and out like a sleeping person. Duration: slow. Easing: always ease-in-out.
Drift, never rush
Background elements drift like smoke or clouds — imperceptibly slow. The ambient glow shifts position over 20–30 second cycles. This creates a sense of depth without drawing conscious attention.
Fade to nothing
Once sound begins, the UI fades to black over 8 seconds. Controls reappear on tap. This is the core UX principle: the app should disappear so sleep can appear.

09 — Brand Principles
Do
  • Speak in short, quiet sentences. Whisper.
  • Use darkness. The dark is the product.
  • Be honest about pricing upfront. Always.
  • Design for 1am. Dim. Slow. Frictionless.
  • Let the UI disappear when not needed.
  • Trust users. They know how to sleep.
  • Use the serif for everything emotional.
Don't
  • Never use the word "journey" or "wellness".
  • Never show ads during sleep or playback.
  • Never ask for an account or email.
  • Never use white backgrounds. Not once.
  • Never use emojis in headlines or copy.
  • Never put a paywall between a user and sleep.
  • Never animate aggressively or bounce.
